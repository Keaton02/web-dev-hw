Lord Alfred Tennyson

Alfred Tennyson was bron in the depths of Lincolnshire, the fourth son of twelve children of the rector of Somersby, George Clyaton Tennyson, a cultivated but embittered clergyman who took out his disappointment on his wife Elizabeth and his brood children -on at least one occasion threatening to kill Alfred's brother. It was in part to escape from the unhappy environment of Somersby rectory that Alfred began writing poetry long before he was sent to school.
